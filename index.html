About the Legend

  • A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, (born - October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, & died - July 27, 2015, Shillong), Indian scientist and politician.
  • He played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs. He was president of India from 2002 to 2007.
  • Sir Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology and in 1958 joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • In 1960, he graduated from the 'Madras Institute of Technology', and joined as a scientist at the 'Aeronautical Development Establishment', after becoming a member of the 'Defence Research and Development Service'.
  • In 1969 He moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation, where He was project director of the SLV-III, the first satellite launch vehicle that was both designed and produced in India.
  • He became the project head of the country's foremost Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III). In July 1980, SLV-III deployed the 'Rohini' satellite successfully near - earth orbit under Kalam's leadership.
  • Kalam wrote several books, including an autobiography, Wings of Fire (1999). Among his numerous awards were two of the country's highest honours, the Padma Vibhushan (1990) and the Bharat Ratna (1997).
  • He left for the heavenly abode on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Shillong. He was laid to rest with full state - honours and his funeral was attended by thousands of people including national - level dignitaries.
